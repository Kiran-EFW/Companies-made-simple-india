## 1. Business Model Overview

### 1.1 What Changed and Why

The original model bundled compliance filing into subscription plans. That model is dead. Here is why:

**The unit economics of compliance-bundled subscriptions do not work.**

| Filing Type | Market Rate (CA Cost) | Our Subscription Price | Margin |
|---|---|---|---|
| GST return (monthly) | Rs 500-1,000/filing | Bundled in Rs 799/mo plan | **Negative** |
| Income Tax Return | Rs 3,000-10,000/filing | Bundled in subscription | **Negative** |
| Annual ROC Filing | Rs 5,000-10,000/filing | Bundled in subscription | **Negative** |

At Rs 799/mo, a customer who needs even one GST return per month costs more to service than they pay. At Rs 1,999/mo, the margin after allocating CA time, filing costs, and customer support is still thin to negative for most segments. The more customers we acquired, the more money we lost on fulfillment.

**The CA firm white-label model is also dead.** No CA firm will pay Rs 999/mo per client for software when they already have Tally, their own workflow, and zero interest in changing. "Making CAs efficient" is not a value proposition CAs care about. They want more clients, not better software. We tried this; it did not work.

### 1.2 The New Model

The new model separates three things the old model conflated:

1. **Incorporation** (one-time transaction, the customer acquisition wedge)
2. **Platform SaaS** (pure software features with 85%+ margins, no fulfillment cost)
3. **Services Marketplace** (professional service providers fulfill work, we retain a platform service margin, we bear no delivery cost)

The fundamental shift: we stop being a compliance fulfillment company and become a **platform company**. Compliance services still happen on our platform, but professional service providers deliver them as marketplace providers, not as our employees or contractors.

**The free hook:** There is no free tier. The only free element is a one-time post-incorporation document bundle -- founders agreement template, first board resolution, and a compliance checklist for your entity type. This is the hook that gets every newly incorporated company onto the Anvils platform. After that, the Starter plan (Rs 499/mo) kicks in for ongoing platform access.

### 1.3 Where Professional Service Providers Fit (They Are Not Customers)

| Old Model | New Model |
|---|---|
| CAs are white-label customers paying Rs 999/mo/client | CAs, CSs, and auditors are marketplace service providers listed for free |
| We sell software to CAs | We send referrals to the right professional for each service |
| CA incentive: "better workflow" (they don't care) | Professional incentive: free lead generation (they care) |
| Revenue: subscription fees from CAs | Revenue: 15-20% service margin on partner-fulfilled services |

**Who does what on the marketplace:**

| Professional | What They Handle | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Chartered Accountant (CA)** | Tax and financial compliance | GST filing, TDS returns, ITR filing, bookkeeping, tax planning |
| **Company Secretary (CS)** | Corporate governance and ROC filings | Annual ROC returns, board minutes, statutory registers, director changes, share transfers |
| **Auditor (must be independent CA)** | Statutory audit | Annual statutory audit (must be a CA who is NOT the company's bookkeeping CA -- independence requirement under the Companies Act) |

These professionals get listed on the Anvils marketplace at no cost. When a company on our platform buys a service (GST filing, ROC annual return, statutory audit), the appropriate professional fulfills it at the agreed partner rate. Anvils charges the client the full service price and pays the partner CA/CS their fulfillment fee. The 15-20% margin between the client price and the partner payout is Anvils's platform service margin. The professional gets a new client they did not have to find. We get margin without bearing any fulfillment cost.

### 1.4 Customer Segmentation (Honest Version)

| Segment | What They Will Pay For | Realistic ARPU | Volume | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Small Private Limited / LLP / OPC | Incorporation + Starter subscription for compliance calendar and reminders. Occasional marketplace services. | Rs 8-15K/yr | High | Starter tier covers dashboard, reminders, document storage. Most stay here. |
| Funded Startups (Pre-Seed to Seed) | Growth subscription (cap table, ESOP, fundraising tools, data room, investor portal). | Rs 42K-1.2L/yr | Low but growing | This is the core high-ARPU SaaS revenue driver. |
| Growth-Stage (Series A+) | Scale subscription (board governance, statutory registers, investor reporting, closing room). | Rs 1.2-2.4L/yr | Very low initially | High ARPU, long sales cycle. Carta alternative positioning. |

**The honest truth about volume:** Most companies incorporating in India are small private limited companies and LLPs. They will pay for Starter (Rs 499/mo) because it gives them a compliance calendar, filing reminders, and document storage -- real ongoing value. But most will not upgrade beyond Starter. The higher SaaS revenue comes from the smaller segment of funded startups who need cap table management, ESOP administration, and fundraising tools -- things they currently pay Carta $100-500/mo (Rs 8,000-40,000/mo) for, or cobble together with spreadsheets.

**Phase 1 entity types (launch):** Private Limited, LLP, OPC only. These three cover 80%+ of startup and SME incorporations. Section 8 (Non-Profit) and Public Limited are Phase 2 additions once the platform is stable and the incorporation workflow is proven.

### 2.2 Revenue Stream 2: Platform SaaS (The Real Business)

This is where the model either works or does not. These are pure software features -- no CA or CS fulfillment cost, no filing cost, no human delivery. Gross margins are 85%+.

**Tier Structure:**

| Tier | Price | Target Segment | Key Features |
|---|---|---|---|
| Free Hook (one-time only) | Rs 0 | Every newly incorporated company | Post-incorporation document bundle: founders agreement template, first board resolution, compliance checklist |
| Starter | Rs 499/mo (Rs 4,999/yr) | All companies on platform | Dashboard, compliance calendar, filing reminders, document storage, company profile, basic analytics |
| Growth | Rs 2,999/mo (Rs 29,999/yr) | Funded startups (Pre-Seed to Seed) | Everything in Starter + cap table management, ESOP administration, fundraising tools, data room, investor portal |
| Scale | Rs 9,999/mo (Rs 99,999/yr) | Series A+ companies | Everything in Growth + board governance, statutory registers, investor reporting, closing room, board portal, multi-entity support |

**Why Starter at Rs 499/mo changes everything:**

In the old model, there was a free tier that kept companies on the platform but generated zero recurring revenue. The problem: companies sitting on the free tier for months or years contributed nothing to the top line. The only revenue came from the small percentage that upgraded to Growth.

With Starter at Rs 499/mo, **every company on the platform generates Rs 5,988/yr in SaaS revenue.** This fundamentally changes the unit economics:

- A company that never upgrades beyond Starter still generates Rs 5,988/yr in pure software revenue at 85%+ margin.
- The free hook (document bundle) gives founders a compelling reason to try the platform. Once they see the compliance calendar and filing reminders, the Starter subscription sells itself.
- Even with high Starter churn (5-8%/mo -- realistic for this segment), the retained base generates meaningful recurring revenue.

**The Growth tier** targets funded startups who need cap table management. Today, their options are:
- **Carta:** $100-500/mo (Rs 8,000-40,000/mo). Excellent product, but expensive and US-focused. India-specific features (ESOP taxation, DPIIT compliance, angel tax) are weak.
- **Spreadsheets:** Free, but error-prone. Breaks when you do a funding round, ESOP grant, or convertible note conversion.
- **Trica / Qapita:** Competitors in this space, but early and not integrated with incorporation and compliance.

At Rs 2,999/mo, Anvils Growth is 60-70% cheaper than Carta and handles India-specific requirements natively. This is a defensible position.

**The Scale tier** targets Series A+ companies with board governance needs. These companies are currently using a combination of Carta + Diligent/BoardEffect + separate data rooms. At Rs 9,999/mo, we consolidate these into one platform.

### 2.3 Revenue Stream 3: Services Marketplace (Platform Service Margin)

Every company on the platform needs compliance services -- GST filing, ITR, ROC annual filing, trademark registration, and more. Instead of fulfilling these ourselves (which destroyed margins in the old model), we list these services at market rates, the appropriate professional fulfills the work, and Anvils charges the client the full service price and pays the partner professional a fulfillment fee, retaining a 15-20% platform service margin.

## 5. Break-Even Analysis

### 5.1 The Path to Break-Even

With lean operations scaling from Rs 2.75L/mo (Phase 1) to Rs 3.75L/mo (Phase 2), break-even requires far less revenue than the old model.

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Phase 1 monthly burn (M1-6) | Rs 2.75L |
| Phase 2 monthly burn (M7-12) | Rs 3.75L |
| Revenue required for break-even | Rs 3.75L/mo (at Phase 2 burn) |
| Revenue composition at break-even | ~Rs 1.25-1.5L incorporation + ~Rs 1.5-1.8L SaaS + ~Rs 0.25-0.5L marketplace |
| Starter subscribers needed | 150-200 |
| Growth subscribers needed | 15-20 |
| Scale subscribers needed | 1-2 |
| Marketplace transactions needed | 30-50/mo |
| **Estimated break-even month** | **Month 12-14** |
| **Cumulative loss to break-even** | **Rs 21-28L** |

**The math:** By Month 12-14, the platform should have 150-200 active Starter subscribers (after churn), 15-20 Growth subscribers, 1-2 Scale subscribers, and a marketplace doing 30-50 transactions/mo. With incorporations running at 50-60/mo, total revenue reaches Rs 3.5-4L/mo. This is the point where revenue meets burn.

If Starter churn is lower than expected (say 3-4%/mo instead of 5-8%), break-even could happen as early as Month 10-11. If churn is higher (10%+), it pushes to Month 14-16. Starter churn is the swing variable.

### 5.2 Break-Even Sensitivity to Key Variables

| Variable | Optimistic | Base Case | Pessimistic |
|---|---|---|---|
| Starter monthly churn | 3-4% | 5-8% | 10%+ |

| Scenario | Break-Even Month | Cumulative Loss |

| Pessimistic (high churn, low conversion) | Month 16-20 | Rs 35-45L |

### 5.3 The Key Drivers

Break-even timing is most sensitive to two variables:

**1. Starter churn rate.** At 5% monthly churn, the retained base compounds nicely. At 10%+ monthly churn, the Starter base erodes faster than new incorporations can replenish it, and the revenue contribution shrinks. Keeping churn below 8% requires the compliance calendar and filing reminders to deliver genuine ongoing value -- companies need to feel that Rs 499/mo is worth it even when they are not actively filing anything.

**2. Growth plan adoption rate.** Each Growth subscriber is worth 6x a Starter subscriber (Rs 2,999 vs Rs 499). Even small changes in the Growth adoption rate have outsized impact on revenue. If 10% of startup-segment companies upgrade to Growth instead of 5-8%, break-even accelerates by 2-3 months.

| Growth Adoption Rate | Break-Even Month |
|---|---|
| 3% of startups | Month 16-18 |
| 5% of startups | Month 13-15 |
| 8% of startups (base case upper) | Month 11-13 |
| 12% of startups | Month 9-11 |

## 7. Risk Analysis

### 7.1 Risk: Starter Churn Is Higher Than Expected

**Probability: Medium-High (40%)**

This is the biggest risk in the new model. Starter at Rs 499/mo only works if companies see ongoing value in the compliance calendar, filing reminders, and document storage. If a company incorporates, downloads their documents, and then sees no reason to keep paying Rs 499/mo, churn will be high.

| Scenario | Monthly Churn | Active Subs at M12 | Starter Revenue at M12 | Impact on Break-Even |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Optimistic | 3-4% | 220-240 | Rs 1.1-1.2L/mo | Break-even at Month 10-11 |
| Base case | 5-8% | 150-175 | Rs 75K-87K/mo | Break-even at Month 12-14 |
| Pessimistic | 10-12% | 90-110 | Rs 45K-55K/mo | Break-even at Month 16-20 |
| Severe | 15%+ | Below 70 | Below Rs 35K/mo | Starter model needs rethinking |

**Mitigations:**
1. **Make the compliance calendar genuinely useful.** Proactive reminders for due dates (GST by 20th, TDS by 7th, ROC annual return, DIR-3 KYC) with enough lead time to act. If a founder avoids even one late-filing penalty because of an Anvils reminder, the Rs 499/mo has paid for itself.
2. **Annual billing at Rs 4,999/yr (17% discount).** Lock in companies for a year. This reduces monthly churn to an annual churn decision. Push annual billing hard during onboarding.
3. **Drip value over time.** Monthly compliance health score, quarterly reminders for board meetings, automated document generation for routine resolutions. Give subscribers a reason to open the platform every month.
4. **If churn exceeds 10% by Month 4, consider repricing to Rs 299/mo or Rs 3,499/yr.** Lower price may reduce churn enough to increase total revenue from the retained base.

### 7.2 Risk: Startup Adoption of Growth Plan Is Lower Than Expected

**Probability: Medium (30%)**

If funded startups do not see enough value in cap table management + data room + investor portal to pay Rs 2,999/mo, the high-ARPU SaaS revenue stream does not materialize. The Starter base provides a revenue floor, but Growth subscribers are what drive the business toward profitability faster.

| Scenario | Growth Adoption Rate | Impact on Break-Even |
|---|---|---|
| Base case | 5-8% of startup segment | Month 12-14 |
| Downside | 2-3% | Month 15-18 |
| Severe downside | Below 2% | Month 18-22 |

**Mitigations:**
1. **Build the product with 5-10 design partners before charging.** Get 5-10 funded startups to use cap table + data room features free for 3 months. Iterate based on their feedback. Only launch the paid tier when retention and engagement are proven.
2. **Focus on the "spreadsheet replacement" use case first.** Most early-stage startups manage their cap table in Excel. The conversion pitch is not "buy our SaaS" -- it is "stop using a spreadsheet that will break when you raise your next round."
3. **Time the upsell to fundraising events.** The moment a startup needs to share a data room with investors or model a funding round, they need these tools. Trigger the Growth upgrade prompt when a company marks "fundraising" as their current stage.
4. **If adoption is below 3% by Month 9, consider a Rs 1,499/mo intermediate tier** with just cap table and basic data room, leaving ESOP and investor portal for the full Growth plan.

### 7.3 Risk: Marketplace Does Not Get Traction

**Probability: Medium (30%)**

Marketplace businesses are notoriously hard to bootstrap because of the chicken-and-egg problem: companies will not use the marketplace if there are not enough CAs and CSs, and professionals will not join if there are not enough companies.

| Scenario | Impact | Probability |
|---|---|---|
| Marketplace reaches 50% of projected transactions | Revenue shortfall of Rs 55K/yr; minor impact on break-even | 30% |
| Marketplace reaches 25% of projected transactions | Revenue shortfall of Rs 83K/yr; break-even delays by 1 month | 15% |
| Marketplace fails entirely | Revenue shortfall of Rs 1.1L/yr; break-even delays by 1-2 months | 5% |

**Why the impact is contained:** Marketplace is only 6-7% of Year 1 revenue. Even if it fails completely, the impact on break-even is 1-2 months, not catastrophic. The marketplace becomes much more important in Year 2-3 as the platform base grows.

**Mitigations:**
1. **Seed the supply side first.** Onboard 10-15 CA firms and 3-5 CS firms before launching the marketplace. Offer them free listings and guaranteed visibility for the first 6 months.
2. **Use incorporation as the demand trigger.** Every company that incorporates immediately needs GST registration, PAN/TAN, bank account opening assistance. Surface these as "next steps" in the post-incorporation flow.
3. **Match the right professional to each service.** Tax work goes to CAs, ROC filings go to CSs, audit goes to independent CAs. Getting this matching right builds trust and repeat usage.
4. **Start with a curated model, not an open marketplace.** Vet and onboard professionals manually. Ensure quality. Expand to open listings only after the curation model proves unit economics.

### 7.4 Risk: Incorporation Volume Does Not Ramp

**Probability: Low-Medium (20%)**

Incorporation is a competitive market. Vakilsearch, IndiaFilings, Cleartax, and dozens of local CA firms offer incorporation services. If we cannot differentiate or acquire customers cost-effectively, volume stays low.

| Scenario | Impact | Probability |
|---|---|---|
| Incorporation volume at 50% of projection | Revenue shortfall of Rs 4.5-5L/yr; Starter subscriber base grows slower; all downstream metrics suffer | 20% |
| Incorporation volume at 25% of projection | Severe impact; platform never reaches critical mass for SaaS or marketplace | 5% |

**Mitigations:**
1. **Differentiate on the post-incorporation experience, not price.** Competitors dump customers after incorporation. Anvils gives them a free document bundle, an immediate Starter subscription with compliance calendar, and ongoing value. This is the pitch.
2. **SEO is the primary acquisition channel.** Long-tail keywords like "how to register a private limited company in India" have high intent and reasonable competition. Invest in content from Day 1.
3. **Free tools as PLG.** Company name availability checker, GST calculator, compliance checklist -- free tools that capture email and drive incorporation.
4. **If CAC exceeds Rs 3,000 by Month 6, pause paid acquisition.** Go all-in on SEO, PLG, and organic. Paid acquisition for incorporation has thin margins and is not sustainable above Rs 2,000-2,500 CAC.

### 7.5 Risk: Carta or Other Well-Funded Competitor Enters India Seriously

**Probability: Low (10-15%)**

Carta has been talking about India expansion for years but has not committed. Qapita and Trica are active but early. If Carta launches in India with full product and US-style pricing, the competitive landscape changes significantly.

**Mitigations:**
1. **India-specific depth is the moat.** ESOP taxation rules, DPIIT compliance, angel tax provisions, RBI regulations for foreign investment -- these are complex India-specific requirements that Carta would take 12-18 months to build properly.
2. **Integration with incorporation is a structural advantage.** A startup that incorporates through Anvils has their cap table pre-populated. Carta cannot offer this because they do not do incorporation.
3. **Price.** At Rs 2,999/mo vs Carta's $100-500/mo, the price gap is wide enough to be a deciding factor for Indian startups.

### 7.6 Risk Summary

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Break-Even Delay | Mitigation Quality |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| High Starter churn | Medium-High | Medium-High | 2-6 months | Moderate (requires product value to be felt monthly) |
| Low Growth plan adoption | Medium | Medium | 2-4 months | Moderate (requires product-market fit iteration) |
| Marketplace no traction | Medium | Low | 1-2 months | Strong (contained impact, multiple fallbacks) |
| Low incorporation volume | Low-Medium | High | 3-6 months | Strong (SEO, PLG, organic channels) |
| Well-funded competitor | Low | Medium | Unpredictable | Moderate (India depth, price, integration) |

### 8.3 Recommended Funding Strategy

**Phase 1 (Month 0-6): Founder Capital Only (Rs 20-30L)**

- Fund the first 6 months entirely from founder capital. At Rs 2.5-3L/mo burn, this requires Rs 15-18L plus buffer.
- Use this period to prove: incorporation volume ramps, Starter conversion and retention are strong, and the compliance calendar delivers enough value to keep churn below 8%.
- At Month 6, you have data: X active Starter subscribers, Y% monthly churn, Z incorporation-to-Starter conversion rate, first Growth subscriber conversions. This is real data, not projections.
- **No external investment needed.** This is a key advantage of the lean model. Founder capital is sufficient to reach the point where the model is validated.

**Phase 2 (Month 6-12): Angel Round Only If Needed (Rs 50L-1 Cr)**

- If Phase 1 metrics are on track (65+ Starter subs, churn below 8%, 4+ Growth subs), the business may not need external capital at all. Revenue at Month 6 should be Rs 1.25L/mo and climbing, against Rs 3.75L/mo Phase 2 burn. The gap is Rs 2.5L/mo, requiring Rs 15-18L to bridge to break-even at Month 12-14.
- If the founder wants to accelerate growth (more marketing, faster hiring, Phase 2 entity types), an angel round of Rs 50L-1 Cr provides runway and growth capital through Month 18-24.
- The pitch: "We have 65 paying Starter subscribers, 4 Growth subscribers, monthly churn of X%, and we are 6 months from break-even on a Rs 3.75L/mo burn. We want to accelerate to Phase 2 entity types and Scale tier enterprise sales."

**Phase 3 (Post Break-Even): Growth from Revenue**

- Once break-even is reached, the compounding takes over. Each new incorporation adds to the platform base, which drives more Starter revenue, marketplace service margins, and Growth upgrades, all with near-zero marginal cost on the platform side.
- Consider raising a Seed round (Rs 3-5 Cr) at this point only if you want to accelerate growth significantly (expand to more cities, add Phase 2 entity types, hire an enterprise sales team for Scale tier, build mobile app).
