# Pricing & Revenue Model

Three revenue streams: Incorporation (one-time platform fee), Platform SaaS (recurring subscriptions for equity/governance tools), and Services Marketplace (per-transaction platform service margin on professionally-fulfilled services).

The free hook is a one-time post-incorporation document bundle (founders agreement template, first board resolution, compliance checklist) provided to every company after incorporation. After that, all platform access requires a paid subscription starting at the Starter tier.

## Revenue Stream 2: Platform SaaS Subscriptions (Recurring)

The SaaS platform is the core business. It provides equity management, fundraising, and governance tools -- NOT compliance filing services. Compliance filing is handled through the marketplace (Revenue Stream 3).

### Platform Tiers

#### Starter Tier
- **Target**: All incorporated companies
- **Monthly**: Rs 499 | **Annual**: Rs 4,999 (save Rs 989)
- **Features**:
  - Company dashboard (entity details, directors, key dates)
  - Compliance calendar with deadline tracking
  - Email and in-app reminders for upcoming filings
  - Document storage (up to 500 MB)
  - KYC tracker (director KYC status, DIN expiry, DSC validity)
  - Access to services marketplace (buy filings a la carte)
  - Company profile page
  - Post-incorporation document bundle (one-time free: founders agreement template, first board resolution, compliance checklist)
- **Purpose**: Gets every incorporated company onto a paid platform from day one. The post-incorporation document bundle is the free hook -- provided once after incorporation to demonstrate value. The Starter subscription then becomes the ongoing operating layer for the company.

#### Growth Plan
- **Target**: Funded startups (pre-seed to seed stage)
- **Monthly**: Rs 2,999 | **Annual**: Rs 29,999 (save Rs 5,989)
- **Features**:
  - Everything in Starter tier
  - Cap table management (shareholders, vesting schedules, waterfall analysis)
  - ESOP pool management and grant tracking
  - Fundraising pipeline and term sheet tracking
  - Virtual data room for due diligence
  - Investor portal (share updates, reports, documents with investors)
  - Convertible note / SAFE tracking
  - Board resolution templates and tracking
- **Why startups pay**: Once you raise money, you need to manage your cap table, track ESOP vesting, and share documents with investors. These are table-stakes tools that funded startups will pay for because getting them wrong has legal consequences.

#### Scale Plan
- **Target**: Series A+ companies with active boards and multiple investors
- **Monthly**: Rs 9,999 | **Annual**: Rs 99,999 (save Rs 19,989)
- **Features**:
  - Everything in Growth plan
  - Board governance suite (agenda builder, minutes, resolutions, voting)
  - Statutory registers (members, directors, charges, share transfers)
  - Investor reporting (automated MIS packs, portfolio dashboards)
  - Closing room for fundraising rounds (document collection, signing workflows)
  - Multi-entity support (subsidiaries, holding structures)
  - Advanced analytics and reporting
  - Priority support with dedicated account manager
- **Why Series A+ companies pay**: Post-Series A, board governance becomes formal. Investors expect quarterly reporting. Statutory registers must be maintained. The complexity justifies the price.

### SaaS Unit Economics

| Metric | Starter Plan | Growth Plan | Scale Plan |
|--------|--------------|-------------|------------|
| Monthly revenue per customer | Rs 499 | Rs 2,999 | Rs 9,999 |
| Cost to serve (infra + support) | Rs 50 - 100 | Rs 300 - 500 | Rs 800 - 1,200 |
| Gross margin | 80 - 90% | 83 - 90% | 88 - 92% |
| Target monthly churn | 5 - 8% | 2 - 4% | 1 - 2% |

No professional time (CA/CS) is required to deliver the SaaS product. It is pure software. This is the fundamental difference from the old compliance-subscription model where CA filing costs consumed all margin.

**Note on Starter churn**: Expect 5-8% monthly churn on Starter subscribers. Many bootstrapped companies will churn after the first few months once they've downloaded their post-incorporation documents and set up basic compliance tracking. Realistic annual retention is 40-55% of Starter subscribers. The key to reducing churn is making the compliance calendar and reminders indispensable -- if they rely on Anvils for deadline tracking, they won't leave.

## Revenue Stream 3: Services Marketplace (Per-Transaction Platform Service Margin)

The marketplace connects companies with partner professionals (CAs, CSs, and auditors) who fulfill compliance and filing services. Anvils charges the client the service price and pays the partner professional a fulfillment fee, retaining a 15-20% platform service margin on each transaction. Companies buy services a la carte -- nothing is bundled into subscriptions.

### How the Marketplace Works

1. **Company** browses available services on the Anvils marketplace
2. **Company** purchases a service (e.g., Annual ROC Filing for Rs 7,999)
3. **Partner professional** (CA, CS, or auditor) is assigned to fulfill the service
4. **Anvils** retains a 15-20% platform service margin (Rs 1,200 - 1,600 on a Rs 7,999 service)
5. **Partner professional** receives a fulfillment fee of 80-85% of the service price

### Professional Partner Model

Three types of professionals fulfill services on the marketplace, each covering distinct domains:

#### Chartered Accountants (CAs)
CAs fulfill tax and financial services:
- GST registration and filing (monthly, quarterly, annual returns)
- TDS quarterly filing
- Income tax returns (ITR for companies, LLPs, individuals)
- Monthly bookkeeping (basic and standard)
- Financial statement preparation
- Tax planning and advisory

#### Company Secretaries (CSs)
CSs fulfill corporate governance and ROC compliance services:
- Annual ROC filings (AOC-4, MGT-7)
- Director appointment and resignation filings
- Share transfer and share allotment filings
- Increase in authorised capital
- Registered office change
- Company name change
- Company closure / strike off
- Maintenance of statutory registers
- Board resolution drafting and filing
- LLP annual filings and partner changes

#### Statutory Auditors
Auditors fulfill statutory audit requirements:
- Annual statutory audit (must be independent from the bookkeeping CA -- the same CA/firm cannot do both bookkeeping and statutory audit for a company)
- Auditor appointment filing (ADT-1)
- Tax audit (if applicable)

All three professional types benefit from:
- **Free listing** on the Anvils marketplace
- **Referral flow**: Companies on the platform need professional services; partners get the work
- **No acquisition cost**: Anvils drives the demand; professionals just fulfill
- **Dashboard tools**: Track assigned tasks, client deadlines, filing status

Anvils benefits from:
- **No professional headcount**: CAs, CSs, and auditors are independent providers, not employees
- **Scalable fulfillment**: More partners = more capacity, no hiring needed
- **Quality control**: Rating system, SLA tracking, automatic reassignment if a partner misses deadlines
- **Regulatory compliance**: Proper separation between bookkeeping (CA) and audit (independent auditor) is maintained by design
