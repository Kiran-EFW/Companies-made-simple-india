## Regulatory Landscape

### Key Regulatory Bodies

| Body | Jurisdiction | Relevance |
|------|-------------|-----------|
| MCA (Ministry of Corporate Affairs) | Company registration, annual filings, director management | Core — all incorporation and ROC filings |
| CBDT (Central Board of Direct Taxes) | Income tax, TDS, PAN/TAN | Tax compliance module |
| CBIC (Central Board of Indirect Taxes) | GST registration, returns, refunds | GST compliance module |
| RBI (Reserve Bank of India) | FEMA compliance, FC-GPR, FLA returns | Foreign investment tracking |
| SEBI (Securities and Exchange Board) | Public company regulations, IPO | Future — public limited support |
| DPIIT (Dept for Promotion of Industry) | Startup India recognition, tax benefits | DPIIT registration service |
| ROC (Registrar of Companies) | State-level company registration | State-wise filing management |

### Key Regulations Driving Demand

1. **Companies Act, 2013** — Mandates annual filings (AOC-4, MGT-7), board meetings (4/year), AGM, statutory audit, director KYC
2. **LLP Act, 2008** — Form 8 and Form 11 annual filings, partner KYC
3. **GST Act, 2017** — Monthly GSTR-1, GSTR-3B returns; annual GSTR-9
4. **Income Tax Act, 1961** — ITR filing, TDS quarterly returns, advance tax, statutory audit
5. **FEMA, 1999** — FC-GPR within 30 days of foreign investment, annual FLA return
6. **Indian Contract Act** — Partnership deed requirements, shareholder agreements
7. **Stamp Act** — State-wise stamp duty on MOA, AOA, share transfers, agreements

### Penalties for Non-Compliance

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Late AOC-4 / MGT-7 filing | Rs 100/day per form per director (no cap) |
| Missing DIR-3 KYC | DIN deactivation + Rs 5,000 fee to reactivate |
| Late GST return | Rs 50/day (CGST) + Rs 50/day (SGST) = Rs 100/day |
| Missing ITR filing | Rs 5,000 - 10,000 + interest on tax due |
| No board meeting in 120 days | Rs 1,00,000 fine on company + Rs 25,000 on each director |
| Missing AGM | Rs 1,00,000 on company + Rs 5,000/day continuing |
| 2 consecutive years non-filing | Company struck off by ROC, directors disqualified for 5 years |

These penalties create strong demand for compliance automation — Anvils' core retention mechanism.

## Differentiators & Moats

### 1. Incorporation-to-Compliance Pipeline
No competitor offers the full journey from entity selection to ongoing compliance in one platform. Once a company is incorporated through Anvils, switching costs for compliance are high (all company data, documents, and history are in the platform).

### 2. Indian Regulatory Depth
28-state stamp duty engine, entity-specific compliance task generation, MCA form auto-filling, Rule 11UA valuations — built for Indian regulations, not adapted from a US/global product.

### 3. AI-Powered Automation
- Entity recommendation wizard
- Document OCR with cross-validation
- Legal document generation from templates
- Copilot assistant with live company context
- Compliance task auto-generation

### 4. Multi-Stakeholder Platform
Founders, CAs, and investors all have dedicated interfaces. CAs bring their clients to the platform (network effect). Investors get portfolio visibility (stickiness).

### 5. Transparent Pricing
Every fee is broken down: platform fee + government fee + stamp duty. State optimization tips help founders choose the cheapest state. This transparency builds trust.

### 6. Recurring Revenue Lock-In
Compliance subscriptions create recurring revenue. The compliance calendar — with penalties for non-compliance — ensures founders don't churn. This is not a nice-to-have; it's a regulatory requirement.
