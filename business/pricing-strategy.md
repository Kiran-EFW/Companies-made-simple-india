# Pricing Strategy — Platform SaaS for India's Startup Ecosystem

Anvils is a platform business, not a compliance services business. We build software tools for equity management, fundraising, and governance. We do not do filings. We track deadlines and sell filing services through a marketplace of partner CAs.

This document covers what we charge, where the margins sit, how we compare to competitors, and why the "Wedge & Upgrade" strategy works.

---

## Part 1: The Model — Why We Changed

### The Old Model (What Failed)

The original plan bundled CA filing services into 8 compliance subscription tiers (Rs 799 - 14,999/month). Every subscriber got "done for you" compliance: ROC filings, ITR, GST, TDS, bookkeeping, and audit coordination.

**Why it failed**: CA professional time is the single largest cost in compliance fulfillment. A Private Limited company's annual filings require 15-20 hours of CA time per year. At Rs 4,999/month (Rs 49,999/year), the CA cost alone consumed Rs 35,000-40,000 -- leaving 15-20% gross margin before any other costs. Lower tiers were outright loss-makers. The Starter plan at Rs 999/month cost Rs 1,170/month to deliver.

This is not a SaaS business. It is a services arbitrage business with thin margins, scaling problems, and no defensibility.

### The New Model (What Works)

Three clean revenue streams with distinct margin profiles:

| Stream | What It Is | Margin | Why It Works |
|--------|-----------|--------|-------------|
| **Incorporation** | One-time platform fee for company formation | 60-70% | Wedge to get companies on platform |
| **Platform SaaS** | Monthly/annual subscription for equity, fundraising, governance tools | 85-92% | Pure software, no professional time needed |
| **Services Marketplace** | Platform service margin on filing services fulfilled by partner CAs | 95%+ | We retain 15-20% service margin, CA does all the work |

**The key insight**: Compliance tracking (calendars, reminders, deadline alerts) is free. It creates engagement and keeps companies on the platform. But equity management, cap table, ESOP, data room, board governance -- these are the tools funded startups will pay for. The money is in equity and fundraising tools, not compliance filing.

## Part 5: The Strategy — "Wedge & Upgrade"

### Core Thesis

Incorporation is the wedge that gets companies onto the platform. The free tier (compliance dashboard) keeps them engaged. When they raise funding, they upgrade to Growth for cap table and ESOP. When they hit Series A, they upgrade to Scale for governance and reporting. At every stage, the marketplace earns service margin on filing services.

```
Incorporation (Rs 1,499-7,999)
    |
    v
Free Dashboard (compliance calendar, reminders, basic docs)
    |
    v  [Company raises pre-seed/seed funding]
    |
Growth Plan (Rs 2,999/mo) — cap table, ESOP, data room, investor portal
    |
    v  [Company raises Series A+]
    |
Scale Plan (Rs 9,999/mo) — board governance, statutory registers, reporting, closing room
    |
    +--- Throughout all stages: Marketplace services (15-20% platform service margin per transaction)
```

### Why "Wedge & Upgrade" Beats "Wedge & Lock"

The old "Wedge & Lock" strategy assumed compliance was the lock -- once a company's filing history was on the platform, they would not leave. But compliance filing is commodity work. A CA can replicate it. There is no real lock-in from filing.

The new "Wedge & Upgrade" strategy creates genuine lock-in through equity data:
- **Cap table history**: Every share issuance, transfer, ESOP grant, and vesting event is recorded. Moving this to a spreadsheet or another platform means re-entering years of transactions.
- **Investor relationships**: The investor portal has shared documents, update history, and reporting dashboards. Investors are already logged into Anvils.
- **Board minutes and resolutions**: Years of governance history in one place. No founder is going to export and re-upload this elsewhere.
- **Data room**: Due diligence documents organized and version-controlled. Changing platforms mid-fundraise is unthinkable.

This is the Carta playbook: once a startup's equity stack is on the platform, switching costs are astronomical.

### Move 1: Make Incorporation the Cheapest in India

**Current pricing**: Rs 1,499 (Launch) for Private Limited -- already 75-80% cheaper than competitors.

**Rationale**:
- At Rs 1,499, our margin is thin (~Rs 500-700 after professional costs), but every incorporation puts a company on the platform for free
- The customer's company data, documents, directors, and shareholder information is now in Anvils from day one
- The free dashboard starts sending compliance reminders immediately, creating a habit
- When this company raises funding (even 6-18 months later), they are already on Anvils and upgrade is one click

**Tactical option**: Offer Rs 0 incorporation for companies that commit to an annual Growth plan (Rs 29,999). This makes the effective CAC = Rs 1,500 (cost of incorporation) against Rs 29,999 in first-year subscription revenue. Outstanding unit economics.

### Move 2: Free Tier as the Engagement Engine

Every incorporated company gets the free tier automatically. No upsell pressure. No trial period. Just a free, useful dashboard.

**What the free tier does**:
- Shows all upcoming compliance deadlines (ROC, ITR, GST, TDS, DIR-3 KYC, AGM, etc.)
- Sends email and in-app reminders 30/15/7/3/1 days before deadlines
- Provides basic document storage (incorporation certificate, MOA, AOA, PAN, TAN)
- Links to marketplace for purchasing filing services

**Why this matters**: India has 50+ compliance deadlines per year for a simple Pvt Ltd. No founder can track these manually. The free dashboard becomes indispensable. Even if a company never pays for a subscription, they keep coming back -- and every time they do, they see marketplace services they can buy.

**Conversion thesis**: 15-20% of companies on the free tier will eventually upgrade to Growth when they raise funding. The free tier is not a cost center -- it is a funnel.

### Move 3: Growth Plan Targets the Real Revenue Pool

India has ~5,000-7,000 startup funding rounds per year (angel, pre-seed, seed). These startups need:
- Cap table management (who owns what after the round)
- ESOP pool setup and grant tracking
- Data room for investor due diligence
- Investor portal for ongoing updates

These are not nice-to-haves. Post-funding, a startup's cap table is a legal document. Getting it wrong creates problems during the next round. Most startups currently manage this in spreadsheets or pay a CS firm Rs 50,000-1,00,000 to maintain it.

The Growth plan at Rs 29,999/year is:
- 70% cheaper than hiring a CS firm for cap table maintenance
- 50% cheaper than Trica Equity's annual plan
- Positioned exactly at the price point where funded Indian startups will pay without hesitation

**Target**: 200 Growth subscribers by Month 18. At Rs 29,999/year each = Rs 60L annual recurring revenue. At 85%+ margin, this is Rs 51L in gross profit.

### Move 4: Scale Plan for High-Value Accounts

Series A+ companies are few (500-800 rounds per year in India) but extremely valuable:
- Rs 99,999/year subscription
- Very low churn (5-10%)
- High marketplace spend (more complex filings, statutory audits, board documentation)
- Multi-year relationships (Series A companies stay on platforms for 5+ years)

**Target**: 30 Scale subscribers by Month 24. At Rs 99,999/year each = Rs 30L ARR. Small number, outsized revenue contribution.

### Move 5: Marketplace as the Long-Tail Revenue Engine

Every company on the platform -- free, Growth, or Scale -- needs filing services. They buy these through the marketplace. Anvils retains a 15-20% platform service margin. The CA does all the work.

**Why this is better than bundling compliance into subscriptions**:
- Zero fulfillment cost for Anvils (CA bears all professional costs)
- Scales infinitely (more CAs = more capacity)
- Revenue grows with the number of companies on the platform, regardless of how many subscribe
- Even free-tier companies generate marketplace revenue

**Marketplace flywheel**:
1. More companies on platform = more service demand
2. More service demand = more CAs want to list
3. More CAs = better service quality and competition
4. Better quality = more companies choose marketplace over random CA
5. Repeat

### Move 6: CAs as Distribution Partners, Not Customers

CAs are not buying our platform. They are listing on our marketplace for free and getting work referred to them. This is a fundamental shift from the old model where we tried to sell CAs on using our platform.

**What CAs get**:
- Free listing on the marketplace
- Inbound service requests from companies already on Anvils
- Dashboard to track assigned tasks, deadlines, and client communication
- No customer acquisition cost -- Anvils drives the demand
- Payment guaranteed (Anvils collects from the company, pays the CA after completion)

**What Anvils gets**:
- No professional headcount
- Distributed fulfillment across hundreds of CAs
- Quality control through ratings, SLA tracking, and reassignment
- 15-20% platform service margin on every transaction without doing any filing work

**CA channel economics**:

| Metric | Direct (Google Ads) | CA Marketplace |
|---|---|---|
| Cost to acquire a company | Rs 4,000-10,000 | Rs 0 (company finds us via incorp/SEO) |
| Cost to fulfill a filing service | Rs 0 (CA does it) | Rs 0 (CA does it) |
| Revenue per service | 15-20% service margin | 15-20% service margin |
| Scalability | Limited by ad budget | Limited only by CA supply |

**Goal**: 100 active CAs on marketplace by Month 12, 300 by Month 24.

## Part 6: The Indian Market Reality

### What Has Not Changed

1. **Price anchoring is low**: Founders expect to pay Rs 5,000-10,000 for incorporation, not Rs 20,000. Our Rs 1,499 Launch tier is well below this anchor -- a major advantage.

2. **Trust matters more than price**: Indian business owners prefer a known CA over an unknown platform. The marketplace model solves this: the company buys from Anvils but is served by a local CA. Trust of the CA + convenience of the platform.

3. **Switching cost is high (once data is in)**: Once a company's cap table, ESOP records, board minutes, and investor documents are on Anvils, switching to spreadsheets or another platform is months of manual work.

4. **Payment behavior**: Indian SMEs prefer annual lump-sum over monthly subscriptions. Both Growth and Scale plans offer meaningful annual discounts.

5. **Regulatory complexity creates demand**: India has 50+ compliance deadlines per year. This complexity drives companies to the free dashboard. The dashboard creates the engagement that fuels marketplace revenue and subscription upgrades.

### What Has Changed in Our Thinking

1. **Compliance is engagement, not revenue**: The old model tried to monetize compliance filing directly. The new model gives compliance tracking away for free and monetizes the tools that funded startups actually need (equity, governance).

2. **CAs are partners, not competitors**: We stopped trying to replace CAs. We give them a marketplace where they get work referred to them. They are our distribution and fulfillment layer.

3. **SaaS margins beat services margins**: A Growth subscription at Rs 2,999/month costs Rs 300-500/month to serve (infra + support). That is 83-90% gross margin. The old compliance subscription at Rs 4,999/month cost Rs 4,250/month to serve. The math speaks for itself.

4. **Funded startups are the real customer**: Bootstrapped companies will use the free tier and buy occasional marketplace services. That is fine -- they generate some service margin revenue. But the real business is funded startups that need equity management tools and will pay Rs 2,999-9,999/month for them.

## Part 8: Pricing Page Recommendations

### How to Display Pricing

**Rule 1: Lead with the free tier**
"Manage your company for free. Upgrade when you raise funding."
This immediately communicates that there is no paywall for basic company management. It builds trust and removes signup friction.

**Rule 2: Position Growth and Scale by use case, not features**
- Growth: "For startups that have raised funding" -- not "cap table management tool"
- Scale: "For companies with active boards" -- not "governance suite"
Founders identify with their stage, not with feature lists.

**Rule 3: Show annual pricing first**
"Rs 29,999/year" not "Rs 2,999/month". Indian customers respond better to a single annual number.

**Rule 4: Marketplace as a separate section**
Do not mix marketplace services into subscription pricing. The subscription page should be clean: Free / Growth / Scale. The marketplace should be a separate "Services" page with all filing services and prices.

**Rule 5: Competitor comparison without naming competitors**
"Other platforms charge Rs 7,000-16,000 for incorporation alone. We start at Rs 1,499."
"Managing your cap table with a CS firm costs Rs 50,000-1,00,000/year. Anvils Growth plan: Rs 29,999/year."

## Part 10: Summary — The Playbook

1. **Incorporation is the wedge.** Price it aggressively (Rs 1,499 Launch for Pvt Ltd). Do not try to make money here. Every incorporation puts a company on the platform.

2. **Free tier is the engagement engine.** Compliance calendars, reminders, and basic docs for Rs 0. Every company uses this. It creates the habit of opening Anvils.

3. **Growth plan is the real business.** Funded startups pay Rs 29,999/year for cap table, ESOP, data room, and investor portal. 85%+ gross margin. This is where the revenue model works.

4. **Scale plan is the premium tier.** Series A+ companies pay Rs 99,999/year for governance, statutory registers, and investor reporting. Small number of customers, large revenue contribution.

5. **Marketplace is the long-tail.** 15-20% platform service margin on every filing service purchased. Scales with platform size. Zero fulfillment cost. Even free-tier companies generate marketplace revenue.

6. **CAs are partners, not competitors.** They list on the marketplace for free, get work referred to them, and Anvils retains a platform service margin on each transaction. No need to hire CAs or manage professional capacity.

7. **Equity data is the moat.** Once a startup's cap table, ESOP grants, investor documents, and board minutes are on Anvils, switching costs are astronomical. This is the real lock-in -- not compliance history, which any CA can replicate.

8. **We compete with Carta for India, not with CAs for compliance.** The competitive frame is equity management and governance tools, not filing services. Vakilsearch and IndiaFilings are in a different business.

9. **Volume target: 200 Growth subscribers + 30 Scale subscribers by Month 24.** This produces Rs 21.5L/month in subscription revenue at 85%+ margin. Combined with marketplace service margins, this makes the business self-sustaining and ready for growth investment.
