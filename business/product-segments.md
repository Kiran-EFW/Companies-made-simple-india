## Part 2: MSME Classification (Updated April 2025)

Source: Ministry of MSME, notification S.O. 1364(E) dated March 21, 2025.

| Category | Investment (Plant & Machinery) | Annual Turnover |
|---|---|---|
| **Micro Enterprise** | Up to Rs 2.5 Crore | Up to Rs 10 Crore |
| **Small Enterprise** | Up to Rs 25 Crore | Up to Rs 100 Crore |
| **Medium Enterprise** | Up to Rs 125 Crore | Up to Rs 500 Crore |

**Key points:**
- MSME classification applies to ANY entity type — Sole Prop, Partnership, LLP, Pvt Ltd, etc.
- Udyam Registration (free, online at udyam.gov.in) is the official classification
- MSME status unlocks: priority sector lending, tender preference, delayed payment protection, reduced patent fees
- **Relevance to Anvils**: MSME is NOT an entity type — it's a CLASSIFICATION applied to existing entities. A Pvt Ltd company can be an MSME. We should offer Udyam registration as a service, not as a separate entity type.

### DPIIT Startup India Recognition

| Criteria | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Entity type | Private Limited, LLP, or Registered Partnership |
| Age | Up to 10 years from date of incorporation |
| Turnover | Up to Rs 100 Crore in any financial year |
| Innovation | Working towards innovation, development, or improvement of products/processes/services |
| Not formed by splitting/reconstruction | Must be a new entity |

**Benefits**: 80IAC tax exemption (3 years), angel tax exemption (Sec 56(2)(viib)), self-certification (6 labor + 3 environment laws), 80% rebate on patent fees, fast-track patent examination, government tender eligibility.

## Part 3: Customer Segments for Anvils

Instead of entity types, think in **customer segments** — grouping by needs, willingness to pay, and feature requirements.

### Segment 1: MICRO-BUSINESS

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Who** | Sole proprietors, small partnerships, freelancers, local shops, home businesses |
| **Entity types** | Sole Proprietorship, Partnership |
| **Annual turnover** | Under Rs 40 lakh (below GST threshold) to Rs 2 Cr |
| **Key need** | "Just help me stay legal" — ITR filing, GST if applicable, basic compliance |
| **Feature set needed** | Incorporation (Partnership deed / GSTIN), ITR filing, GST returns, compliance reminders, basic document storage |
| **Features NOT needed** | Cap table, ESOP, fundraising, investor portal, data room, board meetings, statutory registers, valuations, e-signatures |
| **Willingness to pay** | Rs 500-2,000/month |
| **Volume** | Very high (20+ lakh sole props exist in India) |
| **Margin** | Thin (need automation to be profitable) |
| **Churn risk** | High (will switch for Rs 200/month savings) |

### Segment 2: SME (Small & Medium Enterprise)

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Who** | Established businesses, trading companies, manufacturers, service providers, professional firms |
| **Entity types** | LLP, OPC, Private Limited (non-funded), Partnership (converting to LLP) |
| **Annual turnover** | Rs 50 lakh to Rs 50 Crore |
| **Key need** | "Handle all my compliance so I can focus on my business" — annual filings, GST, TDS, audit |
| **Feature set needed** | All compliance filings (ROC/MCA, ITR, GST, TDS), bookkeeping integration, audit coordination, board meeting templates, basic shareholder management |
| **Features NOT needed** | ESOP, fundraising rounds, investor portal, data room, cap table modeling/dilution, valuations |
| **Willingness to pay** | Rs 2,000-6,000/month |
| **Volume** | High (5+ lakh active Pvt Ltd + LLP companies) |
| **Margin** | Medium (professional time per client is moderate) |
| **Churn risk** | Medium (compliance creates lock-in, but they compare prices) |

### Segment 5: NIDHI COMPANY

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Who** | Mutual benefit societies, micro-finance community groups, member-based savings institutions |
| **Entity types** | Nidhi Company (Public Limited with Section 406 declaration) |
| **Annual turnover** | Rs 10 lakh to Rs 10 Crore |
| **Key need** | "Help me stay compliant with Nidhi Rules — the NDH forms are complex and penalties are severe" |
| **Feature set needed** | Nidhi company incorporation, NDH-1 (statutory compliance return), NDH-2 (extension of time), NDH-3 (half-yearly return of deposits/loans), NDH-4 (declaration application), member management (200+ members), deposit tracking, net owned funds ratio monitoring (1:20), loan management, all standard company compliance (AOC-4, MGT-7, ITR, etc.) |
| **Features NOT needed** | Cap table (shares exist but no active trading), ESOP, fundraising, investor portal, data room |
| **Willingness to pay** | Rs 5,000-15,000/month (compliance burden justifies premium) |
| **Volume** | Low (~1,500-2,000 active Nidhi companies, ~500 new/year) |
| **Margin** | High (niche expertise = premium pricing, few competitors) |
| **Churn risk** | Very Low (no alternatives — CAs charge 2-3x for same service) |

**Nidhi Company compliance details:**

| Filing | Form | Frequency | Deadline | Penalty |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Return of statutory compliance | NDH-1 | Annual | Within 90 days of FY end | Rs 100/day per officer |
| Extension of time (if 200 member threshold not met) | NDH-2 | One-time (if needed) | Before 1 year of incorporation | May be struck off |
| Half-yearly return (deposits & loans) | NDH-3 | Half-yearly | Within 30 days of half-year end | Rs 100/day per officer |
| Declaration as Nidhi | NDH-4 | One-time | Within 120 days of 1 year of incorporation | Cannot operate as Nidhi |
| Annual financial statements | AOC-4 | Annual | Within 30 days of AGM | Rs 100/day per form per officer |
| Annual return | MGT-7 | Annual | Within 60 days of AGM | Rs 100/day per officer |
| Director KYC | DIR-3 KYC | Annual | Sep 30 each year | Rs 5,000 + DIN deactivation |
| Income Tax Return | ITR-6 | Annual | Sep 30 (if audit) | Rs 5,000-10,000 |

**Nidhi operational restrictions:**
- Can ONLY accept deposits from and lend to members
- Cannot issue preference shares or debentures
- Cannot deal in chit funds, hire purchase, leasing, insurance, or securities
- Unencumbered term deposits: minimum 10% of outstanding deposits
- Net owned funds to deposits ratio: 1:20 maximum
- Minimum 200 members within 1 year of incorporation
- Maximum loan: Rs 5 lakh per member (or 2 lakh, per amended rules)
